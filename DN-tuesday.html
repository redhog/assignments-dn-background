Chapter · Role Success

What Matters Most to Succeed

This role delivers value by translating a broad mandate into concrete, measurable outcomes, continuously refined through feedback and impact.

Well-Defined Goals

Clearly defined short- and long-term goals are essential. Without them, the breadth of the role risks becoming unfocused.

Iterative Refinement

Goals should be revisited and refined iteratively—similar to agile development cycles, but applied to the scope and impact of the role itself.

Hard Prioritization for Impact

  • Balance priorities across product teams
  • Minimize duplicate effort and functionality
  • Prefer shared model services over per-team models
  • Identify opportunities for shared libraries

Avoid Overengineering

Solutions should be appropriate for the current team size and maturity—complexity is a cost.

Start with Learning & Low-Hanging Fruit

Learn existing datasets, systems, and processes first. Identify low-effort, high-impact improvements, then course-correct based on outcomes.

Chapter · Previous Experience

Technical Debt: A STAR Story

Situation

Short-term delivery goals had accumulated significant technical debt, while leadership underestimated the resulting development overhead.

Task

Secure leadership buy-in to prioritize technical debt cleanup.

Action

Architectural limitations were translated into monetary cost, framing technical debt as financial debt.

Result

A 20% cleanup budget for one year addressed nearly all technical debt that had been slowing down development.

From Physics to Software: A STAR Story

Situation

A multidisciplinary team of two physicists with limited coding experience, one front-end developer, and myself—with management coming from a physics background rather than software.

Task

Build a production-ready system for geophysics.

Action

  • Taught the Python data science ecosystem and software design principles to the physicists
  • Taught Django to the front-end developer to become full-stack
  • Got the team to adopt an agile development methodology

Result

Delivered a full system for processing, inversion, and visualization of geophysical data within a year.

Chapter · Previous Experience

PhD-Level Code to Production: A STAR Story

Situation

A 12,000-line single-source-file machine learning codebase for geophysics and geotechnics inside a QGIS plugin—written by multiple PhD students over several years, with significant amounts of unused and/or unknown code.

Task

Produce a tool for running data preparation, training, and prediction for production survey projects.

Action

Ran the existing codebase under Coverage during normal use of the software and deleted all unused code—removing 50% of the entire codebase.

Result

A codebase comprehensible in terms of the functionality described by the physicists—a foundation that could be extended into a full standalone tool.

Chapter · Perspective

Open Source & AI for Media

AI Enables New Stories

Especially in large-scale public record analysis, transparency investigations, and structured extraction across decades of archives.

LLMs + Data Science

LLMs extract numerical data from unstructured text; traditional data science then extracts the story from those numbers.

Open Source as Risk Reduction

Code is a necessary liability. Open source spreads maintenance cost and risk across users and contributors.

Synergy & Reuse

Open source enables synergy between otherwise unrelated ventures with overlapping needs.

Reusable Power via DSLs

Domain-specific languages can deliver generic, reusable functionality to non-coding power users—more impactful than one-off code.
