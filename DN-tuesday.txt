- Short summary of what is most important to succeed in this role - maybe even how the role should work practically to provide value
  * Well-defined short- and long-term goals - it's a broad role, so,
    so translating it into concrete, measurable outcomes is essential.
  * Concrete goals should be revisited and refined iteratively -
    similar to agile development cycles, but applied to the impact and
    scope of the role itself.
  * Hard prioritization for impact. Ideally: balance priorities of
    product teams so that duplicate effort and functionality is
    minimized
    - E.g. shared model services over per-team models
    - Identify opportunities to develop shared libraries
  * Avoid overengineering solutions for the team size
  * Start: Learn current datasets, systems and processes, discuss and
    identify opportunities for changes or additions that are low
    hanging fruites. Course correct as needed based on outcome.

- Previous exp. supporting this, use STAR technique

  * Technical debts story
    Situation: Short term delivery goals had accrued a lot of
      technical debts. Leadership understanding of development overhead
      due to this was low.
    Task: Get leadership buy-in on prioritizing cleanup
    Action: Putting monetary value on architectural limitations
    Result: 20% cleanup budget for a year resulted in nearly all
      tech debt slowing down development being adressed.

- Open source and AI for media
  - AI enables stories that would otherwise be practicall impossible -
    especially large-scale public record analysis, transparency
    investigations, and structured extraction across decades of text
    archives.
  - LLMs as a way to extract numerical data from large unstructured
    text corpus like government records, trad. data science to then
    extract the story from those numerical values
  - Code is a necessary liability to deliver functionality - and open
    source limits the liability for each individual user and
    contributor.
  - Open source allows for synergy effects between unrelated business
    ventures that happen to have overlapping functionality needs.
  - Task from last time (textproc) - not the code, but as a story - compare docetl contribution
    - Domain specific languages as a way to deliver reusable and
      generic functionality to non-coding power users
